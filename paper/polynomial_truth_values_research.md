# Polynomial truth values and the degree-valuation loss: research dossier

*Compiled 2026-07-12 from a seven-domain literature sweep (multi-agent, web-verified) plus synthesis.
Context: exploring the replacement of the 0/1 truth encoding in the signed p-adic residual
construction by polynomial truth values — truth = 0, falsity of constraint C = a designated
polynomial f_C(t) — with loss given by the degree valuation (the place at infinity of k(t)).*

## Executive synthesis

**The deflationary core.** The degree map is a homomorphism (k[t]∖{0}, ×) → (ℕ, +). If each
residual only takes the two values {0, f_C(t)}, then "minimize the sum of residual degrees" is
identically weighted MaxSAT with weights w_C = deg f_C — the "nothing is really p-adic" critique
recurs verbatim in tropical form. The polynomial structure earns its keep only through operations
that do NOT factor through the degree: evaluation, factorization, gcd, derivatives, Newton polygons.
Z_p is a DVR with a single finite place (residuals can only report "zero or not"); k[t] has one
place per monic irreducible. That is the load-bearing structural difference.

**Five genuine gains.**

1. *Factorization = conflict diagnosis = provenance.* With falsity tokens (t − α_C)^{w_C}
   (distinct α_C), the master residual (product over violated constraints) factors uniquely:
   root support = which constraints failed (Why-provenance), multiplicities = how badly
   (Trio-level). Ancestor mechanism: the error-locator polynomial of RS/BCH decoding
   (Berlekamp–Massey, Chien search). Formal home: semiring provenance (GKT 2007) — the encoding
   is an evaluation homomorphism out of the free semiring ℕ[X]. No published work combines
   provenance polynomials with a valuation loss: claimable gap.

2. *Conservation law.* Product formula: deg(master residual) = Σ_P deg(P)·ord_P — total falsity
   at infinity = sum of local violations. Artin–Whaples: product-formula fields are exactly
   number fields and function fields, so ℚ-with-primes (current paper) and k(t) (proposal) are
   the only two arithmetics available. The complaint becomes a dichotomy theorem.

3. *Lexicographic priorities, parameter-free.* Falsity t^{w_C} makes level-(w+1) violations
   outweigh any number of level-w ones — the known non-archimedean-weights ≡ lexicographic
   equivalence (Charnes–Cooper 1961; Cococcioni–Pappalardo–Sergeyev 2018). Honest claim per
   Sherali 1982: "instance-independent, tuning-free, overflow-free", not "inexpressible by the
   discrete metric".

4. *Hardness split theorem.* Over F_q[t], degree-minimization of linear systems is
   polynomial-time (minimal approximant bases: Beckermann–Labahn; Forney indices; shifted-Popov
   for weights; SVP over F_q[t] in P per A. K. Lenstra 1985 vs NP-hard over ℤ per Ajtai; Lagarias
   NP-complete Diophantine approximation vs poly-time Padé). Hardness returns exactly when the
   objective counts nonzero residuals (Guruswami–Vardy: ML decoding of RS codes NP-hard; Vardy:
   minimum distance NP-hard). Candidate theorem: the ultrametric layer of the regression is in P;
   all NP-hardness lives in the 0/1 wells (Hamming-type counting, hard in any characteristic).
   Carries: Z_p differs from F_p[[t]] exactly by the carry 2-cocycle (Isaksen 2002), but with
   p > q the current construction never exercises a carry — its hardness is pure CSP encoding.

5. *Verification by random evaluation.* The classical arithmetization payoff (LFKN/Shamir,
   IP=PSPACE): distance amplification cashed by evaluating at a random point (Schwartz–Zippel).
   Concrete: Freivalds-style spot check of a claimed conflict count by one random evaluation of
   the master residual (soundness error deg/|F|); plausibly a sum-check-style verification of the
   loss floor without 2^n enumeration. The 0/1 encoding cannot support this.

**Roots and factorization — semantics.**
Roots = places = constraint identities. Multiplicity = severity; divisibility by (t−α)^w ⇔
prescribed derivatives to order w−1 (Guruswami–Sudan interpolation-with-multiplicities;
multiplicity codes). Newton polygon = violation spectrum readable without factoring; Hensel slope
factorization splits the residual by severity class. Kronecker-substitution weights make the
scalar degree losslessly decodable into the full violation multiset (small novel theorem: the
loss value IS complete provenance). gcd of master residuals across runs/instances = shared
failure modes via the Euclidean algorithm. Alon–Tarsi graph polynomial ∏(x_u − x_v) is the
classical master residual, always evaluated to a scalar (destroying factor identity); the
proposal's delta is refusing to evaluate.

**Design caveats (must handle in any construction).**
(a) deg(0): use |r| = q^{deg r} with |0| = 0; define the master residual over violated
constraints only. (b) Nonzero constants have degree 0 — the loss must charge every nonzero
residual (q^{deg} does: q^0 = 1). (c) Sums of residuals acquire spurious roots at undesignated
places — build multiplicatively or bound the phantom mass (honest lemma). (d) Small fields:
need |k| ≥ #constraints for distinct roots, else distinct irreducibles (degree doubles as
weight). (e) Escaping the indicator degeneracy: encode clause falsity multiplicatively over
literals so deg(clause residual) = weighted count of falsified literals — a graded
distance-to-satisfaction the 0/1 metric cannot express, changing the local-search landscape.

**Positioning against prior art.**
Gröbner/ideal Sudoku encodings (Gago-Vargas et al. 2006; Arnold–Lucas–Taalman 2010): decision +
counting, all-or-nothing; the degree loss fills the graded-violation gap. Proof complexity:
NS/PC degree is a different quantity (certificate side of the duality; different variables);
weighted Polynomial Calculus (Bonacina–Bonet–Levy 2023; Bonacina–Levy 2025) is the ready-made
dual certificate system (wPC derivation of s weighted copies of 1 certifies loss ≥ s); degree
lower bounds (Razborov; Lauria–Nordström 2017; Conneryd et al. FOCS 2023) foreclose algorithmic
speedup claims. Tropical: deg is a tropicalization; tropical linear regression ≡ mean-payoff
games ∈ NP ∩ co-NP (Akian–Gaubert–Qi–Saadi 2023) — locating the k[t] regression between
P / NP∩co-NP / NP-hard is open. Soft-CSP semirings (Bistarelli–Montanari–Rossi 1997): pitch as
the same CSP annotated higher up the provenance hierarchy. Ultrametric design space: Ostrowski
for k(t) classifies all places; the degree place is the unique global aggregator; the full
design space is a Berkovich tree with the trivial (discrete) metric at the root — the current
construction sits at the root, the proposal moves out along the infinity ray; multivariate
version: term orders are a slice (Robbiano), coset-minimization is Gröbner normal form
(Chan–Maclagan for valued coefficients), hardness localizes at coset-vs-variety.

**Top three paper-worthy moves.**
1. The split theorem (ultrametric layer Popov-easy; hardness in the wells; reduce from
   Guruswami–Vardy).
2. Factorization-as-provenance (error-locator semantics; Kronecker weights → lossless scalar
   loss).
3. The Schwartz–Zippel loss verifier (implementable in the playground; the one capability the
   discrete metric provably lacks).

**Further leads.**
- Unique-decoding regime for MaxCSP: an assignment with degree loss below half the minimum
  designated-zero weight is the unique minimum-conflict assignment in its Hamming ball
  (Johnson-bound transfer) — explains/predicts when local search near the floor is safe.
- Coefficients of the master residual = elementary symmetric polynomials of the violated
  tokens (moments of the violation set); summing over assignments gives signed enumeration
  invariants in the Alon–Tarsi spirit (thematically tight: solved Sudokus are Latin squares).
- (k[t]/units, gcd, ×) is a commutative semiring ≅ product of tropical semirings indexed by
  irreducibles — arguably the right carrier for the full provenance theorem.
- Bodirsky–Fehm anomaly: valuation-constrained linear systems NP-complete for p ≥ 3, in P for
  p = 2 — check whether k = F_2 (Zhegalkin territory) makes the polynomial construction easier.
- Open problem worth posing: the function-field analogue of Deng–Luo–Xiao's p-adic LVP/CVP under
  adversarial norms — poly-time supports the "carries cause hardness" thesis, hardness refutes it.
- Mihara digitwise section: in F_q[t] digits are genuinely independent (carry-free), so digitwise
  noise models are natively equal-characteristic — a remark for the existing Mihara discussion.

## Domain: algebraic-proof-complexity

Algebraic proof complexity has, for thirty years, treated exactly the quantity the paper is circling: the DEGREE of polynomial certificates of constraint violation. The Nullstellensatz proof system (Beame-Impagliazzo-Krajicek-Pitassi-Pudlak, Proc. LMS 1996) certifies unsatisfiability of a polynomial system {f_i} by exhibiting multipliers g_i with sum g_i f_i = 1, and its complexity measure is the maximum degree (in the problem variables x) of the g_i f_i. Polynomial Calculus (Clegg-Edmonds-Impagliazzo, STOC 1996) is the dynamic version: it proved that a degree-d refutation, if one exists, is FOUND by a degree-bounded Groebner basis computation in time n^O(d), making certificate degree the tractability dial for algebraic SAT solving. Degree lower bounds are the field's core results: Razborov (1998) showed PHP needs PC degree > n/2 over any field; Impagliazzo-Pudlak-Sgall (1999) established the size-degree relation (high degree forces exponentially many monomials); Lauria-Nordstrom (CCC 2017) showed graph k-colouring needs linear PC degree, killing hopes that Groebner/Nullstellensatz methods (including De Loera et al.'s NulLA algorithm, which literally computes the minimum-degree Nullstellensatz certificate by incrementing d and solving a linear system at each d) beat resolution-based solvers on colouring. And de Rezende-Goos-Nordstrom-Pitassi-Robere-Sokolov (STOC 2021) proved that finding NS/PC refutations in time polynomial in the shortest refutation is NP-hard — proof search is hard even when low-degree certificates exist.

Against this backdrop the SEED CONJECTURE is REFUTED AS STATED but refines into something better. The proposal's loss is the degree in the auxiliary variable t of the residual r_C(t), which for each constraint is either 0 (satisfied) or the fixed polynomial f_C(t) (violated). So the loss is sum over violated C of deg f_C — a weighted conflict count with integer weights w_C = deg f_C. This is NOT Nullstellensatz/PC degree, for two structural reasons. (1) Different variables: NS/PC degree is multivariate degree in the problem variables x of certificate multipliers; the proposal's degree is univariate degree in t, i.e., a valuation-at-infinity acting as a per-constraint weight. (2) Different objects: a low-loss solution is an ASSIGNMENT (witnessing an upper bound on min-conflicts); an NS/PC certificate is a polynomial identity valid at all points (witnessing a lower bound). They sit on opposite sides of a duality. The complexity gap confirms non-identity: minimum NS certificate degree is found by linear algebra in n^O(d) (NulLA), while minimizing the proposed loss is NP-hard weighted MaxCSP. The correct proof-complexity reading of the proposal is instead the WEIGHTED Polynomial Calculus line: Bonacina-Bonet-Levy (SAT 2023) define PC with weights in N or Z where deriving s weighted copies of the polynomial 1 soundly and completely certifies MaxSAT cost >= s, and Bonacina-Levy (SAT 2025) extend this to full completeness for MaxCSP over any finite field with native multi-valued encodings (Thm 3.6 soundness, Thm 5.1 completeness). The degree-valuation loss with weights w_C = deg f_C is exactly a wPC cost function; wPC is the ready-made sound-and-complete dual certificate system for the proposed regression, and NS/PC degree (in x) then re-enters as the complexity parameter governing how hard those cost lower bounds are to find (n^O(d) by CEI96, with linear-degree obstructions by Lauria-Nordstrom and NP-hardness of proof search by de Rezende et al.).

On what polynomial-valued truth buys: the literature says the ENCODING of finite domains into an algebra changes proof complexity by exponential factors, so the choice of f_C is mathematically substantive, not cosmetic. Bonacina-Galesi-Lauria (MFCS 2022 / comput. complexity 2023) show that constraints over roots of unity (the natural multiplicative analogue of the proposal's per-constraint roots t - alpha_C) generate instances hard for both PC and sum-of-squares in degree AND size; Dahiya-Mahajan-Mouli (SAT 2024, DOI 10.4230/LIPIcs.SAT.2024.10) show +/-1 vs 0/1 encodings change PC size bounds from exp(Omega(d^2/n)) to exp(Omega(d)); Bonacina-Levy's F_3 example (x != y encoded as x^2+xy+y^2-1) shows direct multi-valued encodings are exponentially more concise than Boolean translations. Also note: Boolean Groebner bases (PolyBoRi, Brickenstein-Dreyer, J. Symbolic Computation 2009) already work in the polynomial ring F_2[x]/(x^2-x), so "truth values living in a polynomial ring" is the existing baseline — the genuinely new ingredients in the proposal are (a) polynomial-valued TARGETS with the valuation-at-infinity loss, (b) per-constraint distinct falsity polynomials, and (c) the master-residual observation. For (c): if the alpha_C are distinct, the divisor (multiset of roots) of the master residual identifies exactly WHICH constraints are violated — factorisation is conflict diagnosis. I found no precedent for this in proof complexity (nearest neighbour: Groebner-based unsatisfiable-core extraction), and I found NO prior work anywhere treating proof degree as an ML regression loss — searches for that combination return nothing relevant, which supports novelty but also means the seed conjecture has no existing scaffolding. One warning transfers verbatim: since each residual still takes only two values {0, f_C(t)}, the valuation on k[t] again acts as a (weighted) zero/nonzero indicator — the paper's own "nothing is really p-adic" critique recurs for k[t] unless the construction makes deg r depend on the assignment in a richer way than violated/not-violated.

### Connections

- **[high]** The degree-valuation loss is exactly a weighted MaxCSP cost with clause weights w_C = deg f_C, and weighted Polynomial Calculus (Bonacina-Bonet-Levy 2023; Bonacina-Levy 2025) is a sound and COMPLETE certificate system for precisely this cost over finite fields — the paper gets a dual proof system almost off the shelf: a wPC derivation of s weighted copies of 1 from the compiled polynomials certifies loss >= s, and completeness guarantees the exact optimum is always derivable.
  - *Relevance:* Gives the proposal its correct proof-complexity reading (replacing the refuted seed conjecture) and a candidate new paper section: 'the dual of the degree-loss regression is weighted Polynomial Calculus'.
- **[high]** Seed conjecture refuted as stated: the proposal's residual degree is univariate degree in the place variable t (a weight), while Nullstellensatz/PC degree is multivariate degree in the problem variables x of certificate multipliers; moreover a low-loss SOLUTION is an assignment (upper-bound witness) whereas a low-degree certificate is an identity over all assignments (lower-bound witness). The two quantities also have mismatched complexity: min certificate degree is linear-algebra computable in n^O(d), min loss is NP-hard.
  - *Relevance:* Directly answers the seed conjecture: 'structurally the same quantity' is false; the refined true statement is the wPC-duality above, with NS/PC degree as the search-complexity parameter, not the loss value.
- **[high]** Proof degree HAS been used as an explicit optimization objective — but over certificates, not assignments: De Loera-Lee-Malkin-Margulies's NulLA algorithm computes the minimum-degree Nullstellensatz certificate by fixing degree d, solving a linear system for the multiplier coefficients, and incrementing d on failure; it proved non-3-colorability of graphs with thousands of nodes.
  - *Relevance:* Closest existing precedent to 'minimising a polynomial degree that certifies constraint violation'; the paper can cite it as the certificate-side twin of its assignment-side degree loss, and reuse its incremental-degree linear algebra as an algorithm for verifying regression lower bounds.
- **[medium]** Clegg-Edmonds-Impagliazzo's n^O(d) Groebner proof search gives the paper a tractability frontier: whenever the compiled system admits degree-d (in x) refutations/cost certificates, the decision problem 'loss >= s' has certificates findable in quasi-polynomial time for small d — so the hardness of the regression localizes in instances requiring high certificate degree.
  - *Relevance:* Enables a new theorem section: parameterized upper bounds for the regression's verification problem, complementing the existing NP-hardness result.
- **[high]** Linear degree lower bounds are known for exactly the paper's instance classes: Lauria-Nordstrom show bounded-degree non-k-colourable graphs need PC degree Omega(n) (hence exponential size) under both the Groebner encoding and De Loera et al.'s Nullstellensatz encoding, and Razborov shows PHP needs degree > n/2 over any field.
  - *Relevance:* Caps any claim that the polynomial reformulation makes Sudoku/colouring-type compiled instances algebraically easy; the paper can import these as unconditional lower bounds on its dual certificates.
- **[high]** Even when low-degree/short algebraic certificates exist, FINDING them is NP-hard (de Rezende-Goos-Nordstrom-Pitassi-Robere-Sokolov, STOC 2021, for both Nullstellensatz and PC).
  - *Relevance:* Parallels and strengthens the paper's NP-hardness framing: not only is the regression NP-hard, but the natural certificate-search dual is non-automatable — worth citing to preempt 'just search for certificates' objections.
- **[medium]** Per-constraint falsity polynomials (t - alpha_C) are the additive analogue of roots-of-unity domain encodings, and the literature proves encoding choice changes proof complexity exponentially: vanishing sums of roots of unity are hard for PC and SOS in degree and size (Bonacina-Galesi-Lauria), and +/-1 vs 0/1 Boolean encodings change PC size lower bounds qualitatively (Dahiya-Mahajan-Mouli, SAT 2024).
  - *Relevance:* Answers 'what does polynomial-valued truth buy': the choice of falsity polynomial is a genuine degree of freedom with provable complexity consequences, giving the paper a principled reason to study f_C beyond aesthetics.
- **[medium]** With distinct alpha_C, the master residual (product of per-constraint residuals) has divisor equal to the multiset of violated constraints — factorisation of one polynomial recovers the entire conflict set, and root multiplicities aggregate shared-root constraints into weights. No precedent found in proof complexity; the nearest existing notion is Groebner-basis-based extraction of unsatisfiable cores.
  - *Relevance:* This is the most plausibly NOVEL contribution the polynomial-valued construction enables: an algebraic conflict-diagnosis theorem ('the loss is the degree of the master residual; its roots name the violated constraints'), which 0/1 truth values cannot express.
- **[high]** Boolean Groebner bases (PolyBoRi) already place truth values in the polynomial ring F_2[x]/(x^2-x) and are the practical engine for algebraic SAT solving, so 'truth in a polynomial ring' per se is the existing baseline, not the novelty.
  - *Relevance:* Sharpens the paper's novelty claim: what is new is polynomial-valued targets plus the valuation-at-infinity (degree) loss and per-constraint falsity polynomials — the paper should position against PolyBoRi/ANF explicitly.
- **[high]** The 'nothing really p-adic' self-critique recurs for k[t]: since each residual takes only the two values {0, f_C(t)}, the degree valuation again acts as a weighted zero/nonzero indicator, and any weight function w_C would do — unless the construction is changed so deg r varies with the assignment (e.g., degree encodes distance-to-satisfaction), making the ultrametric genuinely load-bearing.
  - *Relevance:* Identifies the precise condition under which the polynomial upgrade escapes the paper's own objection; this is analysis grounded in the wPC literature (weights in N suffice for completeness), not in any single citation.

### Open questions

- Can the wPC_kappa completeness theorem (Bonacina-Levy 2025, Thm 5.1) be instantiated to give the paper a formal duality theorem: 'the minimum degree-valuation loss of the compiled regression equals the maximum s such that s weighted copies of 1 are wPC-derivable from the compiled polynomials, with weights w_C = deg f_C'? This looks nearly off-the-shelf but needs the compilation's unary-well terms translated into the finite-field polynomial format wPC expects.
- Can the construction be modified so the residual degree varies with the assignment beyond the binary {0, deg f_C} — e.g., deg r encodes distance-to-satisfaction or the number of falsified literals — so that the valuation at infinity is genuinely load-bearing and the paper's own 'nothing is really p-adic' critique no longer applies to k[t]? Does any such modification change the problem's approximability class?
- Is there a proof-theoretic counterpart to the master-residual identity (sum of residual degrees = degree of the product): does the factorisation of the master residual into per-constraint factors correspond to any decomposition of wPC/Nullstellensatz certificates, or to Groebner-based unsatisfiable-core extraction? Also worth checking against the Ideal Proof System's single-certificate viewpoint (Grochow-Pitassi, JACM 2018 — NOT verified this session, confirm before citing).
- Do known PC degree lower bounds (Razborov's PHP, Lauria-Nordstrom's colouring) transfer to lower bounds on the SIZE of weighted-PC cost certificates for the compiled MaxCSP instances — i.e., is certifying 'loss >= s' as hard as certifying plain unsatisfiability for these families?
- Is automatability of weighted Polynomial Calculus (finding minimal-cost wPC derivations in time polynomial in the shortest one) NP-hard, analogously to de Rezende et al. STOC 2021 for unweighted NS/PC? I found no published result either way — a genuine open question the paper could pose or attack.
- No prior work was found that treats algebraic proof DEGREE as a machine-learning loss function (multiple searches combining proof/certificate degree with loss/objective/regression returned nothing relevant). This supports a novelty claim, but an independent literature pass on the learning-theory side (e.g., neural proof search guided by degree heuristics) should confirm the gap.

## Domain: sudoku-groebner-nullstellensatz

The polynomial-system literature on Sudoku and colouring does exactly one thing the current paper's p-adic encoding also does, and does it with more algebraic infrastructure: it identifies satisfying assignments with the variety of an ideal. The canonical Sudoku encoding (Gago-Vargas, Hartillo-Hermoso, Martin-Morales, Ucha-Enriquez, CASC 2006; Arnold-Lucas-Taalman, CMJ 2010) uses one variable per cell, a univariate domain polynomial F(x_j) = prod_{i=1..9}(x_j - i) pinning each cell to its finite domain, and for each pair of cells that must differ the divided-difference polynomial G(x_i,x_j) = (F(x_i)-F(x_j))/(x_i-x_j). This is the exact ideal-theoretic counterpart of the paper's "unary wells" plus "forbidden hyperplanes": the domain polynomial is the generator of the vanishing ideal of the domain (Alon's Combinatorial Nullstellensatz Theorem 1.1 says polynomials vanishing on a grid S_1 x ... x S_n lie in the ideal generated by g_i = prod_{s in S_i}(x_i - s), with degree control). What Groebner bases then buy is decision and counting, not optimisation: the reduced Groebner basis is {1} iff the puzzle is unsolvable (weak Nullstellensatz), is {x_i - a_i} for a proper (unique-solution) Sudoku, and since the ideal is radical, dim_Q Q[x]/I counts solutions exactly (Gago-Vargas et al. verified 288 Shidoku boards this way). Crucially, everything is all-or-nothing: an assignment either lies on V(I) or it does not; there is no native notion of "how many constraints are violated".

Two strands of this literature nevertheless attach quantitative meaning to a degree, and they are the works against which the proposed "degree valuation as ultrametric loss" must be delimited. First, the Alon-Tarsi strand: the graph polynomial prod_{(u,v) in E}(x_u - x_v) is literally a "master residual" — a product with one factor per constraint, where the factors vanishing at an assignment are exactly the violated constraints — but it is always EVALUATED in a field, so the product collapses to a single scalar and the identity/multiplicity of vanishing factors is destroyed; the polynomial method instead extracts global information from a COEFFICIENT (Alon-Tarsi 1992: the coefficient of the top monomial is a signed count of Eulerian orientations, giving list-colourability; specialised to Latin squares this becomes the Alon-Tarsi conjecture, directly relevant since Sudoku boards are Latin squares). Second, the Nullstellensatz-certificate strand (De Loera-Lee-Margulies-Onn CPC 2009; De Loera-Lee-Malkin-Margulies ISSAC 2008/JSC 2011, the NulLA algorithm): infeasibility is certified by 1 = sum beta_i f_i, certificates of fixed degree are found by linear algebra, and the minimum certificate degree is a meaningful invariant — provably >= 4 for non-3-colourability, and for stable sets exactly equal to the stability number alpha(G), with at least one term per stable set. So "degree carries combinatorial quantity" is NOT new per se; what is new in the proposal is that degree is a PER-ASSIGNMENT loss (a regression objective whose minimisers are minimum-conflict assignments), whereas Nullstellensatz degree is a per-INSTANCE proof-complexity measure defined only when the system is fully infeasible. A targeted search found no prior use of the degree valuation / place at infinity of k(t) as a loss function for CSP violation counting.

The seed conjecture is therefore CONFIRMED in outline but needs two refinements. Refinement 1 (weakens the novelty claim on the loss): deg is a valuation, so deg(prod r_C) = sum deg(r_C) with no possibility of cancellation in the integral domain k[t]; hence with falsity tokens t^{w_C} the ultrametric loss is IDENTICALLY the weighted conflict count — the same "nothing is really p-adic/polynomial about the loss value" objection the authors already raised recurs, now in max-plus/tropical form (deg is precisely the tropicalisation map). What weights do add over the current 0/1 scheme is expressiveness: weighted MAX-SAT objectives and lexicographic constraint hierarchies via w_C = q^j come for free. Refinement 2 (strengthens the novelty claim on structure): the genuinely new algebra is not the loss value but the FACTORISATION. Z_p is a discrete valuation ring with a single finite place, so a residual can only report "zero or not" — this is the precise formalisation of the authors' own complaint. k[t] has one place per monic irreducible: choosing distinct tokens f_C = (t - alpha_C) gives each constraint its own place, and the finite-place valuations v_{t-alpha_C}(R) of the master residual R (equivalently gcd computations, or evaluations R(alpha_C)) recover exactly WHICH constraints are violated and with what multiplicity, while the place at infinity (degree) aggregates them. Nothing in the Groebner/Nullstellensatz literature reads violated constraints off residual factorisation this way — there the analogous diagnostic role is played by the certificate coefficients beta_i or by primary decomposition of the solution ideal. Finally, hardness transfers and must be cited honestly: linear degree lower bounds for Nullstellensatz/polynomial calculus refutations of colouring (Lauria-Nordstrom CCC 2017; worst case), on average for sparse random graphs over any field (Conneryd-de Rezende-Nordstrom-Pang-Risse FOCS 2023), and degree Omega(girth) bounds (Romero-Tuncel) foreclose any claim that the polynomial reformulation makes SOLVING easier; the contribution can only be structural/expressive, exactly like the NP-hardness framing already in the paper.

### Open questions

- Can the master residual arise as an actual regression residual (y - <w, x> in a k[t]-module model) rather than being defined as a product post hoc? In the current proposal residuals multiply by fiat; in genuine regression residuals ADD, and sums of token polynomials can cancel or produce spurious low-degree residuals, breaking the identity 'loss = weighted conflict count'. Determining when additive residual structure preserves or destroys the factorisation read-off seems to be the central technical question.
- Is there an ultrametric analogue of the De Loera-Lee-Margulies-Onn stable-set theorem, e.g. 'the minimum over cofactors h_i of deg(R + sum h_i g_i), reducing the master residual modulo the grid ideal, equals the minimum conflict count'? This would bridge per-assignment loss and Nullstellensatz-certificate degree and would be a genuinely new theorem type for the paper.
- What do the coefficients of the master residual R(t) = prod over violated C of (t - alpha_C) mean when summed over all assignments (or over a solver's trajectory)? They are elementary symmetric functions of the violated tokens, so one gets signed/moment enumeration of violation patterns — is there an Alon-Tarsi-style theorem where nonvanishing of a specific coefficient of the total sum certifies satisfiability?
- Does the shape-lemma/counting machinery (reduced Groebner basis = {x_i - a_i} for unique solutions; vdim counts solutions via radicality, as in Gago-Vargas et al.) have any counterpart in the degree-loss framework, e.g. can the number of minimum-loss assignments be read off an algebraic invariant of the encoding rather than by enumeration?
- How exactly do proof-complexity lower bounds transfer? Deciding 'degree loss = 0' is graph colouring, so NP-hardness is immediate, but do the LINEAR-DEGREE lower bounds (Lauria-Nordstrom; Conneryd et al.) formally rule out natural relaxation hierarchies (fixed-degree linear algebra in t) for approximating the minimum degree loss itself?
- The product formula on k(t) (the sum over all places of the valuation, weighted by residue degree, is zero for any nonzero rational function) links the degree at infinity to the finite-place valuations at the tokens (t - alpha_C). Can this be turned into a clean duality statement in the paper — aggregate loss at infinity equals total violation mass at the finite places — and does choosing tokens of higher residue degree (irreducible f_C of degree w_C) give anything beyond integer constraint weights?
- Prior encodings work modulo the grid vanishing ideal (generators g_i = prod_{s in D_i}(x_i - s), per Alon's Theorem 1.1), which normal-forms all data to per-variable degree < |D_i|. Should the proposed construction likewise be formulated in the quotient ring k[t][x]/(grid ideal), and does the degree valuation descend sensibly to that quotient?

## Domain: boolean-arithmetization-coding

Arithmetization is exactly the classical precedent for "truth values expanded into polynomials": LFKN (J. ACM 1992) and Shamir's IP=PSPACE replace a Boolean formula by a low-degree polynomial over a larger field that agrees with it on {0,1}^n, and Thaler's textbook treatment makes the payoff explicit — the Reed-Solomon/low-degree-extension encoding is "distance-amplifying": two vectors differing in a single coordinate have encodings differing on a 1-(n-1)/p fraction of points. Crucially, that payoff is only cashed in by EVALUATING the polynomial at a random point of a large field (Schwartz-Zippel). Seed conjecture (a) is therefore half right: the k[t] proposal IS an arithmetization (Boolean discrepancy -> nonzero polynomial residual), but as currently specified it never evaluates anything — the loss is deg(r), and deg is a homomorphism (k[t]\{0}, x) -> (Z>=0, +). Summing degrees factors through that homomorphism, so the objective collapses to a weighted conflict count, and the paper's own "nothing is really p-adic" critique recurs verbatim as "nothing is really polynomial." What polynomial truth genuinely buys, per the literature, are the operations that do NOT factor through deg: evaluation (Freivalds/Reed-Solomon fingerprinting of the master residual — a probabilistic O(1)-query verifier for the MaxCSP objective), factorization (which constraints failed, not just how many), and derivatives/multiplicities. Aaronson-Wigderson's algebrization barrier is the standard caveat on overclaiming what arithmetization can do.

Seed conjecture (b) needs refinement. The paper's existing wells (I checked paper/sudoku_padic_regression.tex: Lemma "Unary wells", weight lambda = 1 + Delta dominating the negative rewards) are weighted penalty OBSERVATIONS, not vanishing conditions on any interpolating polynomial — so "structurally the same primitive as Guruswami-Sudan" is only an analogy for the current mechanism. But for the NEW proposal the correspondence becomes exact: if falsity of constraint C is encoded as (t - alpha_C)^{w_C} with distinct alpha_C, then the degree loss is the total multiplicity of designated zeros of the master residual, and the governing inequality — total multiplicity of zeros of a nonzero polynomial is at most its degree — is precisely the primitive underlying Guruswami-Sudan interpolation (multiplicity r at a point imposes (r+1 choose 2) constraints inside a weighted-degree budget, buying decoding radius 1 - sqrt(R)) and Kopparty-Saraf-Yekhanin multiplicity codes (codewords are evaluations of polynomials AND their derivatives, distance via the multiplicity Schwartz-Zippel lemma). So (b) is true of the proposed construction, not of the existing p-adic wells; stating it that way is both more defensible and more interesting, because GS's second step (factor the interpolant to LIST candidates) suggests the genuinely novel move: factoring the master residual is a decoding step.

Seed conjecture (c) is confirmed as mathematics — a nonzero degree-d univariate polynomial over a field has at most d roots counted with multiplicity, which is the Reed-Solomon distance argument — but with two load-bearing caveats. (i) It needs residuals confined to {0} union {designated falsity polynomials}: an unconstrained predictor can emit a nonzero CONSTANT residual (degree 0), violating a constraint at zero degree-loss, so "degree loss upper-bounds Hamming/MaxSAT loss" fails unless the loss charges every nonzero residual at least 1 (e.g. loss |r| = q^{deg r} with |0| = 0, rather than loss = deg r), or the encoding forces violated residuals to be the designated polynomials. (ii) deg(0) must be handled as -infinity/zero-cost, and "sum of degrees = degree of the product" holds only when all residuals are nonzero — one satisfied constraint annihilates the master residual, so the master residual should be the product over VIOLATED constraints only, or one works with the valuation v(0) = +infinity. Under the intended encoding the conjectured inequality is actually an equality (loss = sum of w_C over violated C), and the at-most-d-roots direction is most valuable read backwards, as a decoding statement: the master residual is literally an error-locator polynomial in the Berlekamp-Welch/RS-decoding sense — its roots at the designated points alpha_C locate the violated constraints, and its degree counts them with multiplicity. That gives the paper three defensible new sections: (1) certificate semantics — factor the master residual to name the violated constraints; (2) a Schwartz-Zippel spot-check protocol — verify a claimed conflict count by evaluating the master residual at one random field element, soundness error deg/|F|, in the style of RS fingerprinting and sum-check verification of the loss; (3) weighted MaxCSP via multiplicities w_C with GS-flavored threshold theorems. Two adjacent literatures must be cited to defend novelty: polynomial calculus (Clegg-Edmonds-Impagliazzo 1996), where Boolean constraints are already polynomial equations and DEGREE is the canonical complexity measure, and Bonacina-Levy (SAT 2025), which already extends polynomial calculus to MaxCSP over many-valued variables — the closest existing "algebraic degree meets MaxCSP" work I found. The approximate-degree literature (Bun-Thaler survey) is a further, more speculative connection: if predictors were restricted to low-degree polynomials of the inputs, dual-polynomial lower bounds could witness unavoidable loss.

### Open questions

- Loss convention: is the loss deg(r) (a nonzero constant residual then costs 0 and can hide a violation, breaking conjecture (c)'s inequality) or the absolute value q^{deg r} with |0|=0 (constants cost 1)? And how is deg(0) handled, since 'sum of degrees = degree of the product' fails the moment one residual is exactly zero — should the master residual be the product over violated constraints only, or should the paper work with the valuation v(0)=+infinity?
- Does the polynomial layer survive its own 'nothing is really p-adic' test? deg is a homomorphism (k[t]\{0},x)->(Z>=0,+), so a loss that only sums degrees is isomorphic to weighted MaxSAT with integer weights w_C; the construction earns 'polynomial' status only if the paper actually uses evaluation, factorization, gcds, or derivatives of the master residual. Which of these will it commit to?
- Can the Schwartz-Zippel spot-check be developed into a full verification protocol — e.g., a sum-check-style delegation where a prover claims the conflict count of an assignment and a verifier checks it with O(1) evaluations of the master residual at random field elements — and is that new relative to existing algebraic MaxSAT verification (check Bonacina et al. 2023/2024 on polynomial calculus for MaxSAT, cited by the SAT 2025 paper)?
- Do Guruswami-Sudan/Johnson-bound thresholds transfer to meaningful CSP statements, e.g., 'any assignment with degree loss below half the minimum designated-zero weight is the unique minimum-conflict assignment in its Hamming ball', and is there a nontrivial list-decoding regime for near-optimal assignments?
- Is the minimum achievable degree loss related to Nullstellensatz or polynomial-calculus refutation degree of the underlying CSP — in particular, can PC degree lower bounds be repurposed as lower bounds on any proof that the loss floor is what the paper claims?
- If per-constraint falsity polynomials (t-alpha_C)^{w_C} are used, distinct alpha_C require |k| >= number of constraints (or an extension field): does the field-size requirement interact with the paper's finite-domain encoding, and would evaluation points in an extension of F_q suffice as in Reed-Solomon practice?

## Domain: function-field-analogy-lattices

The function-field analogy delivers exactly the sharp algorithmic dichotomy the paper needs, and the literature is mature enough to cite chapter-and-verse. Over F_q[t] with the degree valuation (the place at infinity of F_q(t)), lattice problems that are NP-hard over Z are polynomial-time: A. K. Lenstra (1985) introduced "reduced bases" (orthogonality defect zero, i.e., sum of basis-vector degrees equals the degree of the determinant) and a polynomial-time reduction algorithm that yields shortest vectors in F_q[x]-lattices; Ivanyos-Kutas-Ronyai state the contrast explicitly: shortest vector over F_q[x] is polynomial-time while "the analogous problem is NP-hard in the case of integer lattices" (citing Ajtai, STOC 1998). The reason is structural, not merely algorithmic: in the ultrametric, orthogonality defect 0 is always achievable (Mahler's 1941 function-field geometry of numbers has Minkowski-type theorems with equality, successive minima attained by a basis), whereas over Z the defect is unavoidable and reduction is only approximate. The computer-algebra community has industrialized this: Mulders-Storjohann's weak Popov form gives simple polynomial-time row reduction with applications explicitly including short-vector computation and linear-system solving; Beckermann-Labahn's order/approximant bases and their descendants (Giorgi-Jeannerod-Villard ISSAC 2003, O~(n^omega sigma); Jeannerod-Neiger-Schost-Villard, O~(m^(omega-1)(sigma+|s|)) for SHIFTED minimal interpolation bases) solve "find all minimal-degree solutions of a linear system over k[t]" in softly-linear time. Forney (1975) supplies the invariant-theoretic layer: a minimal basis of a rational vector space minimizes the SUM of row degrees, that minimum is a module invariant (Forney/minimal indices), and row-reduced bases achieve it — which is precisely the proposal's "total loss = sum of residual degrees = degree of the master residual" objective in its unconstrained form.

Seed conjecture (a) is CONFIRMED for the linear layer, with a sharpening. Minimizing residual degrees of a linear system over k[t] — including per-constraint weights, since falsity encoded as t^(w_C) is literally a "shift" in the shifted-Popov/shifted-minimal-basis sense — is the minimal approximant/interpolation basis problem and is solvable in quasi-linear time. Moreover the coding-theory tradition shows this is not a fringe fact but THE workhorse of algebraic decoding: Fitzpatrick (1995) recasts the Reed-Solomon key equation as finding minimal elements (under term orders capturing degree) of a solution module, unifying Berlekamp-Massey and Euclid as degree-minimisation; Alekhnovich (FOCS 2002 / IEEE-IT 51(7):2257-2265, 2005, verified) solves minimal-degree linear Diophantine systems over polynomials in near-linear time for soft RS decoding. Crucially, the hardness boundary INSIDE the function-field world is also known precisely: the moment the objective stops measuring degrees and starts COUNTING nonzero residuals, NP-hardness returns — Guruswami-Vardy (2005) prove maximum-likelihood decoding of Reed-Solomon codes (= minimize the NUMBER of violated evaluation constraints) is NP-hard, and Vardy (1997, IEEE-IT 43:1757-1773, verified via IEEE Xplore doc 641542) proves minimum Hamming distance of a linear code is NP-hard. This is the sharpest possible version of the paper's own self-criticism: the paper's construction only ever uses the 0/1 indicator (conflict counting), which is Hamming-type and hard in ANY characteristic; the genuinely ultrametric part (degree minimisation) is Popov-easy. In the k[t] version one can state and prove a clean split theorem: unconstrained degree-loss regression is in P (approximant bases), and NP-hardness appears exactly when finite-domain wells force the loss to degenerate into an indicator/counting objective.

Seed conjecture (b) — "hardness of integer p-adic regression is attributable to carries" — needs refinement in two directions. Supporting it: the crispest theorem-pair is Lagarias (1985), who proved good simultaneous Diophantine approximation over Q is NP-complete (SIAM J. Comput. 14:196-209), versus its function-field analogue (simultaneous/Hermite-Pade approximation), polynomial-time by Beckermann-Labahn — same problem shape, carrying side hard, carry-free side easy. The carries themselves have a precise algebraic formalization: Isaksen (Monthly, 2002) shows the carry map is a 2-cocycle classifying Z/p^2 as a nontrivial extension of Z/p (while F_p[t]/t^2 is the trivial extension); in Witt-vector language (Rabinoff, arXiv:1409.7445, verified to exist) Z_p = W(F_p) differs from the equal-characteristic F_p[[t]] exactly by the carry polynomials. Against the strong form of (b): first, the paper's own construction deliberately sets p > q so that no arithmetic ever wraps a digit — its NP-hardness never exercises carries, so carries cannot explain the existing paper's hardness (which is pure CSP-encoding hardness, present identically over k[t]); second, Deng-Luo-Xiao (ePrint 2018/1229, published J. Syst. Sci. Complexity) introduced Longest Vector and Closest Vector Problems for p-adic lattices under general norms, for which only exponential deterministic algorithms are known and on which public-key schemes were built (ePrint 2021/522, published in p-Adic Numbers Ultrametric Anal. Appl. 2024) — partially broken by Zhang (arXiv:2409.08774: deterministic poly-time LVP when the field is totally ramified and p is polynomial in the rank), but not in general. So ultrametric lattice problems can be hard on the mixed-characteristic side for reasons of adversarial bases/norms rather than carries per se, and the honest refined claim is: carry-freeness plus the degree valuation gives exact, polynomial-time reduction theory (defect zero), which is what makes the k[t] regression's continuous part tractable; hardness can be reintroduced on either side by counting objectives (wells) or adversarial norms.

## Domain: nonarchimedean-ml-optimization

The single most load-bearing finding for the proposal is that the seed conjecture is essentially CORRECT and its provenance is traceable, but it needs one refinement. The equivalence between non-Archimedean weighted scalarization and lexicographic optimization has two established layers. (a) The operations-research tradition: preemptive ("non-Archimedean") priority levels in goal programming go back to Charnes & Cooper (1961) and Ijiri (1965), where priority factors P_1 >> P_2 are formally symbols such that no finite multiple of P_2 exceeds P_1 — i.e., infinitesimal-weight scalarization avant la lettre. (b) The modern rigorous/computational layer: Cococcioni, Pappalardo & Sergeyev (Appl. Math. Comput. 318, 2018) prove that weighting the i-th objective of a lexicographic multi-objective LP by an infinitesimal ①^{-(i-1)} (grossone methodology) yields a single-objective non-Archimedean LP whose optima are exactly the lexicographic optima, solvable by a generalized simplex ("GrossSimplex"); Fiaschi & Cococcioni extend this to interior-point and quadratic settings. The refinement: Sherali (EJOR, 1982) proved that for LPs (and any finite instance) there always EXIST finite "equivalent weights" realizing the lexicographic order — so for a finite CSP the discrete metric with integer weights (e.g., weights growing like (m+1)^level) CAN express lexicographic priorities. What the valuation genuinely buys, per the explicit argument in Cococcioni & Fiaschi's "Big-M with the numerical infinite M" (Optim. Lett. 2021), is an instance-independent, tuning-free, overflow-free encoding: finite equivalent weights depend on the instance and blow up numerically, while a non-Archimedean weight (grossone ①^w, or the proposal's t^{w_C} under the degree valuation) is the same for every problem and is exact. The paper should therefore claim "natively and parameter-free", not "inexpressible by the discrete metric".

The second major connection is that the proposal's degree-valuation loss is, structurally, a TROPICALIZATION. The degree of a polynomial is minus the valuation at the place at infinity of k(t), and deg(r1·r2) = deg r1 + deg r2 is exactly tropical (max-plus) multiplication — the proposal's "master residual" identity is the statement that the valuation map is a semiring homomorphism onto the tropical semiring. This puts the construction directly on top of a mature literature: Allamigeon–Benchimol–Gaubert–Joswig showed tropical polyhedra/LP are precisely images under the valuation of LP over real Puiseux series (a field of the same family as k((1/t))) and built a tropical simplex method; Akian–Gaubert–Qi–Saadi proved tropical linear regression is polynomial-time equivalent to mean payoff games (hence in NP ∩ co-NP, a strictly finer complexity statement than generic NP-hardness); Maragos et al. surveyed tropical/max-plus regression and morphological perceptrons in Proceedings of the IEEE; Zhang–Naitzat–Lim proved ReLU networks ARE tropical rational maps. So "regression over k[t] with degree loss" has a ready-made algorithmic and complexity toolbox, and a genuine risk/opportunity: the authors must check whether their construction, after applying the valuation, collapses to a known tropical regression problem — if it does not (because the CSP structure forces residuals into a discrete set of polynomials rather than a continuum), that difference is itself a result worth stating.

On the p-adic/ultrametric ML side, the niche is real but sparse, and valuation-GRADED losses are only just appearing: Martins ("Learning with the p-adics", arXiv:2512.22692, Dec 2025) builds classification/regression foundations over Q_p and shows compact p-adic representations impossible over R; Mihara (p-Adic Numbers Ultrametric Anal. Appl. 18(1), 2026) formulates p-adic optimization as the "infinitesimal limit of the least squares method" — the closest published relative of a valuation-graded loss — and notes the embedded max-feasible-subsystem problem over F_p is APX-complete; v-PuNNs (arXiv:2508.01010) do "valuation-adaptive" optimization over Z_p; Zúñiga-Galindo's p-adic statistical field theory / Boltzmann-machine series and Murtagh's ultrametric data-analysis program supply the broader context. Bodirsky/Fehm (arXiv:2504.13536) give the sharpest complexity anchor: solvability of linear systems over Q_p or Z_p with valuation constraints v_p(x)=c is NP-complete for p ≥ 3 but polynomial-time for p = 2, while one-sided constraints v_p(x) ≥ c or ≤ c stay polynomial — a template for how valuation constraints, not the field itself, generate hardness, which is exactly the paper's own diagnosis of its current construction. Finally, lexicographic objectives are an active demand-side topic in ML proper (Skalse et al., IJCAI 2022, lexicographic multi-objective RL with convergence proofs), so a loss that realizes lexicographic priorities inside a single scalar valuation has an audience beyond CSP encodings. One technical caution to pass to the authors: the "sum of degrees = degree of the product" identity requires all residuals nonzero; satisfied constraints should therefore leave a UNIT residual (degree 0), not a zero residual, or the master-residual formulation degenerates.

### Open questions

- Does the k[t]-degree regression, after applying the valuation map, reduce exactly to a known tropical regression problem (Maragos et al. / Akian-Gaubert-Qi-Saadi)? If yes, the paper's contribution must live at the 'lift' level (factorization, roots, per-constraint encodings); if no, characterizing the gap is itself a theorem.
- Complexity placement: plain conflict-count loss is NP-hard, but tropical linear regression is in NP ∩ co-NP via mean payoff games. Where does the lexicographically graded degree loss sit, and do Bodirsky-Fehm-style dichotomies (exact vs one-sided degree constraints; p=2 vs p>=3 analogues over small fields k) carve out tractable fragments?
- Roots and factorization semantics of the 'master residual' appear to be unclaimed in this literature: e.g., with falsity encoded as (t - alpha_C), the set of violated constraints is readable from the roots of the master residual, and the Newton polygon of the residual (valuation at 0 vs degree at infinity) could grade two independent priority hierarchies simultaneously. No prior work found doing this — verify against coding-theory domains (Reed-Solomon syndrome decoding looks structurally identical).
- Attribution nuance for the paper's related-work section: secondary sources credit Charnes & Cooper (1961) and Ijiri (1965) with preemptive/'non-Archimedean' priorities in goal programming, but I could not verify from primary sources whether the term 'non-Archimedean' appears in the 1961 book itself; a primary-source check is needed before citing it for the terminology (Sherali 1982 and the grossone papers are safe citations for the equivalence).
- Degenerate-case check: if all constraint weights w_C are equal, the degree loss collapses to an affine function of the conflict count — exactly the weakness the authors already flagged for the p-adic version. The lexicographic grading (distinct w_C levels) is therefore the essential new ingredient; the paper should also compare against existing 'lexicographic/weighted partial MaxSAT' formulations (e.g., Boolean lexicographic optimization, Marques-Silva et al.), which I did not have time to verify in depth.
- Technical convention: 'sum of residual degrees = degree of the product of residuals' fails if any residual is the zero polynomial (deg 0 = -infinity). Satisfied constraints must leave unit residuals (degree 0), i.e., truth should be encoded so residuals are units, not zero — otherwise the master-residual identity and the multiplicative structure degenerate.
- Is there any prior work using DIFFERENT falsity polynomials per constraint, e.g. (t - alpha_C) with distinct evaluation points? Nothing surfaced in the non-archimedean-optimization literature; the nearest relatives are grossone monomial weights (one 'axis' per priority level) and polynomial interpolation/CRT encodings, suggesting this specific idea may be novel to this domain.

## Domain: valuation-geometry-ultrametric-space

The valuation geometry of k(t) gives the proposal something the current Z_p construction provably lacks: a structured, completely classified space of ultrametrics with a conservation law. The function-field Ostrowski theorem (Conrad's blurb gives a self-contained proof) says the ultrametric absolute values on k(t) trivial on k are exactly the finite places |.|_P, one per monic irreducible P, plus the place at infinity |r| = c^{deg r}. This immediately answers "what other ultrametrics are there" and singles out the degree loss canonically: a finite place assigns every polynomial norm <= 1 and detects only violations divisible by its own prime, while the place at infinity is the unique place at which every nonconstant residual is visibly "false". The product formula (deg of a principal divisor is zero; axiomatized by Artin-Whaples 1945) is precisely seed conjecture (a): deg(master residual) = sum over finite places of deg(P) * ord_P(residual). Conjecture (a) is therefore CONFIRMED as stated mathematics, with one refinement the paper must own: the sum runs over ALL finite places, so the ledger "total falsity at infinity = sum of designated local violations" is exact only when the encoding guarantees residuals factor entirely into the constraint-designated factors; sums (as opposed to products) of residuals can acquire spurious roots at undesignated places, which appear in the ledger as phantom violations. Newton polygons (verified from Kedlaya's 18.787 notes: slopes = valuations of roots with multiplicity; slope multisets add under multiplication) then give factorisation semantics: assigning constraint C the falsity token (t - alpha_C)^{w_C} or t^{w_C} makes the master residual's Newton polygon a violation spectrum — which severity classes were violated, with what multiplicity — readable from coefficient valuations without factoring, and Hensel slope factorization splits the residual canonically by severity. This is a concrete answer to questions (1) and (2): polynomial truth values buy graded, localizable, multiplicatively additive falsity, versus the single bit the trivial ultrametric provides.

Seed conjecture (b) is HALF right and the correct version is sharper than the conjecture. The true statements: (i) every term order is a real-weight-matrix order (Robbiano 1985), i.e., a higher-rank monomial valuation, so term orders are a slice — not all — of the ultrametric design space; (ii) minimizing a term-order-compatible ultrametric over an affine coset f + I is exactly Groebner normal-form computation (the normal form is the coset representative with minimal leading monomial — an elementary lemma), and Chan-Maclagan (Math. Comp. 2019) extend Buchberger/normal-form machinery to coefficients carrying a valuation, which is exactly the proposal's situation of k[t]-valued residuals measured by degree; (iii) the set of monomial ultrametrics decomposes into finitely many polyhedral cells on which the initial data of the constraint ideal is constant — the Groebner fan (Mora-Robbiano 1988) for trivially valued coefficients, and the more general Groebner COMPLEX (Maclagan-Sturmfels GSM 161, ch. 2) when coefficient valuations are nontrivial, with the tropical variety sitting inside as the subfan of weight vectors admitting no leading-order infeasibility certificate (Bogart-Jensen-Speyer-Sturmfels-Thomas). The false part: "choosing the ultrametric = choosing a term order". The space of all multiplicative ultrametric seminorms on k[x_1..x_n] over trivially valued k is the Berkovich analytification, which is vastly larger than the set of term orders (already for k[t] it is an R-tree with a ray for each closed point plus the degree ray — Jonsson's notes; for two variables the valuative tree of Favre-Jonsson). Payne's theorem repairs the conjecture: the analytification is the inverse limit of all tropicalizations, so the full design space is the limit of the polyhedral (Groebner-fan-level) shadows rather than any single fan. Kaveh-Manon's Khovanskii-basis theory then delimits exactly which non-term-order valuations still support finite Groebner-type normal-form computation.

For the paper, this domain contributes three publishable ingredients. First, a uniqueness/justification theorem: among all places of k(t), the degree place is the unique one seeing all violations, and by Artin-Whaples any loss design with an exact product-formula conservation law forces a global field — so Q-with-primes (the current paper) and k(t)-with-irreducibles (the proposal) are essentially the only two arithmetics available, turning the authors' "nothing is really p-adic" complaint into a dichotomy. Second, a conservation-law section: total degree loss decomposes exactly into local violation orders (with the spurious-root caveat as an honest lemma/obstruction), plus a Newton-polygon "violation spectrum" for weighted MaxSAT variants. Third, a design-space section: the loss regimes of the regression are parameterised by the Groebner complex of the lifted constraint ideal (finitely many polyhedral regimes; walls = phase transitions of the objective), embedded in a Berkovich tree/space whose root recovers the trivial-ultrametric degenerate case — with the coset-vs-variety distinction cleanly separating the polynomial-time layer (normal form = regression over an affine coset) from the NP-hard layer (minimisation over the domain-well variety).

It also yields a clean uniqueness theorem justifying the proposal's choice: a finite place pi satisfies |r|_pi <= 1 for every polynomial r, with strict inequality iff pi divides r — so each finite place 'sees' only violations divisible by its own prime; the place at infinity is the UNIQUE place (up to equivalence) at which every nonconstant residual has norm > 1, i.e., the unique loss under which all falsity tokens are simultaneously visible. ### Open questions

- Can the encoding force the master residual's zero divisor to be supported only on constraint-designated places (no spurious roots), e.g. by using products rather than sums of per-constraint residuals, so that the product-formula ledger 'degree at infinity = sum of designated local violations' is exact — and if not, can spurious-root mass be bounded or given a semantic role (interaction terms between constraints)?
- Where exactly is the complexity jump between 'minimise degree loss over an affine coset f + I' (Groebner normal form, polynomial time once a basis is fixed — and the univariate domain polynomials prod_{c in D_i}(x_i - c) already form a Groebner basis under suitable orders) and 'minimise over the domain-well variety' (NP-hard)? A theorem localizing hardness at coset-vs-variety would sharpen the paper's reduction.
- Does any non-monomial point of the Berkovich design space (e.g. a quasi-monomial valuation or a valuation along an exceptional divisor) yield a regression objective that is qualitatively different from all term-order losses — different minimisers, different approximation ratio, or a different complexity class — or does the Groebner-fan finiteness collapse them all into finitely many regimes?
- With coefficients in k[t] carrying the degree valuation, what exactly is the Groebner complex of the lifted constraint ideal for standard CSP families (Sudoku, list-colouring, small CNF)? Are its cells computable with Chan-Maclagan's algorithms or the gfan/tropical machinery, and do wall-crossings correspond to interpretable phase transitions of the loss landscape?
- The product formula on k(t) and on Q are the only two options by Artin-Whaples rigidity — does the k(t) version admit anything the Q version cannot, e.g. residue-field independence (choose k = F_2 vs k = Q), places of arbitrary degree as 'compound constraints', or Frobenius/automorphism actions permuting designated places, that would give the polynomial encoding provable advantages over the p-adic one rather than just aesthetic ones?
- Is there a faithful-tropicalization statement for the regression: a single (finite) embedding whose tropicalization already distinguishes all loss regimes relevant to the CSP, so the inverse limit in Payne's theorem is not needed in practice?

## Domain: graded-truth-provenance-semantics

This domain delivers the strongest theoretical scaffolding found so far for the k[t] proposal, because "polynomials as truth values whose monomial/factor structure IS the explanation" is not a metaphor here — it is a fully developed theory. Green–Karvounarakis–Tannen (PODS 2007) annotate database facts with elements of an arbitrary commutative semiring and show positive query evaluation lifts to annotations; crucially, the semiring N[X] of provenance polynomials is the FREE commutative semiring on the fact-tokens X (their Prop. 4.2: every valuation v: X → K extends to a unique homomorphism Eval_v: N[X] → K), so evaluation in ANY semiring factors through provenance polynomials (their Thm. 4.3: q(R) = Eval_v ∘ q(R̄)). Monomials record which facts a derivation uses, exponents record how many times, coefficients count derivations. The Karvounarakis–Green survey arranges the coarser provenance models in a surjection hierarchy: B[X] drops coefficients, Trio(X) drops exponents, Why(X) drops both, Lin(X) collapses to one monomial. Grädel–Tannen then extended semiring provenance from databases to full first-order logic with negation via quotient semirings of polynomials with DUAL indeterminates (a token for each literal and its negation), explicitly motivated by "explaining missing query answers or failures of integrity constraints" — i.e., provenance of falsity, which is exactly the semantic role the residual polynomial plays in the proposal; in the game version, provenance values describe the number and properties of winning strategies. Finally, the tropical semiring T = (N ∪ {∞}, min, +) is the standard COST specialization: Deutch–Milo–Roy–Tannen state verbatim that a homomorphism into T "corresponds to associating numeric values (costs) with base facts; then the provenance of derived facts is the minimal cost of a derivation."

SEED-CONJECTURE VERDICT: CONFIRMED, with one load-bearing refinement and two caveats. Because the paper's total loss is the sum of residual degrees = the degree of the PRODUCT of residuals, only the multiplicative structure of k[t] is exercised. The master residual is then precisely the image of a single provenance monomial ∏_C x_C^{e_C} (e_C = violation multiplicity) under the evaluation homomorphism x_C ↦ (t − α_C)^{w_C} guaranteed by GKT universality. Unique factorization in k[t] inverts this map when the α_C are distinct: the root SUPPORT of the master residual is exactly Why(X)-provenance of failure ("which constraints"), and root MULTIPLICITIES restore the exponents ("how badly") — Trio/N[X]-level information. The degree is then the further specialization into the tropical cost semiring (deg(fg) = deg f + deg g, i.e., deg is a monoid homomorphism (k[t]\{0}, ·) → (N, +), and it is exactly the valuation at infinity). So the proposal reconstructs, inside one univariate polynomial ring, three distinct rungs of the established provenance hierarchy: k[t]-with-roots ≙ Why/Trio provenance, degree ≙ tropical cost, and the current paper's 0/1 discrete metric ≙ the Boolean semiring B — a precise, citable answer to the authors' own objection that "nothing is really p-adic" in the 0/1 version. Caveat 1: this reading survives ONLY multiplicative aggregation; polynomial ADDITION destroys roots ((t−α)+(t−β) = 2t−(α+β) has neither root), so if the regression pipeline ever sums residual polynomials the provenance object degrades to its degree bound. The clean fix is to observe that (k[t], gcd, ·) modulo units is itself a commutative semiring (gcd(ac,bc) = gcd(a,b)·c in a UFD) isomorphic to a product of tropical semirings indexed by irreducibles — arguably the "right" carrier for a genuine semiring-provenance theorem. Caveat 2: distinct roots need |k| ≥ #constraints; over small fields one can instead assign distinct IRREDUCIBLE polynomials per constraint (factorization still works, and irreducible-degree doubles as a constraint weight).

Two adjacent bodies of work both enable and temper novelty claims. Enabling: nobody appears to have combined provenance polynomials with a valuation/degree loss — repeated searches for provenance semirings + ultrametric/p-adic/valuation losses return nothing (a genuine gap the paper can claim). Weight design is also solved off the shelf: Kronecker substitution (mapping multivariate monomials injectively to univariate degrees via x_i ↦ t^{D^{i-1}}) shows how to pick constraint weights w_C so that the SCALAR degree alone losslessly decodes the entire violation multiset — turning even the tropical image into complete provenance. Tempering: the core mechanism "roots of a designated polynomial identify violated positions, found by root search" is classical coding theory — it is exactly the error-locator polynomial of Reed–Solomon/BCH decoding (Chien search finds the roots; roots = error locations) — so the paper should cite this as the ancestral mechanism while claiming the new use (regression loss whose residual is a provenance object for CSP violation). Additional precedent to cite for "ring-valued truth" generally: Zhegalkin (1927) showed Boolean logic IS polynomial arithmetic over Z/2 (algebraic normal form), and Löwe–Tarafder's generalized algebra-valued models of set theory show whole set-theoretic universes can take truth values in an algebra — though neither attaches a valuation/loss to the algebra. Finally, Bistarelli–Montanari–Rossi's semiring-based CSP (JACM 1997) already frames soft/weighted CSPs as semiring-valued constraint problems: the paper's minimum-conflict objective is the weighted-CSP (tropical) instance of that framework, and the k[t] proposal is best presented as annotating the SAME CSP in a more informative semiring higher up the provenance hierarchy, from which the loss is recovered by homomorphism — a framing with 30 years of citable machinery behind it.

### Open questions

- No published work appears to combine provenance semirings with an ultrametric/p-adic/degree valuation as a LOSS function (multiple searches returned nothing) — is this gap real, or hiding under other names (e.g., 'cost semiring lifting', weighted model counting with structured weights, or the Giansiracusa universal valuation semiring in tropical geometry)?
- The provenance reading of the master residual survives only multiplicative aggregation, since polynomial addition destroys roots. Is (k[t]/units, gcd, ·) — which is a commutative semiring in any UFD, isomorphic to a product of tropical semirings indexed by irreducibles — the correct carrier for a full semiring-provenance theorem about residual encodings, and does the regression pipeline ever force genuine addition of residuals?
- Distinct roots α_C require |k| ≥ #constraints. Over small/finite fields, does assigning distinct IRREDUCIBLE polynomials per constraint (with irreducible-degree doubling as constraint weight) preserve both the factorization-provenance theorem and the NP-hardness reduction, and does char 2 (Zhegalkin/F_2[t]) give any additional algebraic simplification?
- Is decoding the violation set from the master residual (Berlekamp–Massey/Chien-search style, by analogy with error-locator polynomials) ever computationally cheaper than re-evaluating the constraints directly — e.g., when the residual is maintained incrementally, transmitted in compressed form, or produced by an oracle/solver whose internals are opaque?
- Can Grädel–Tannen dual indeterminates be imported so that SATISFIED constraints also contribute informative (nonzero, non-unit) factors — giving the residual gradient-like structure near satisfaction instead of collapsing to 0 — without breaking the 'global minimisers = minimum-conflict assignments' theorem?
- Does the Kronecker-weight construction (w_C as radix weights so the scalar degree losslessly decodes the violation multiset) interact badly with the hardness reduction or with numerical/complexity bounds, since weights grow exponentially in the number of constraints?
- Is there a formal relationship between the k[t]-residual construction and Shapley-value/responsibility attribution of facts to query answers (Livshits–Bertossi–Kimelfeld–Sebag line of work), i.e., can root multiplicities be given a causal-attribution semantics rather than just a counting one?
