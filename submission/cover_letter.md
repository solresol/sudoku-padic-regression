Please consider the manuscript "Signed p-adic Residual Encodings of Finite-Domain All-Different Systems with a Sudoku Case Study" for publication in *p-Adic Numbers, Ultrametric Analysis and Applications*.

The paper gives a signed p-adic residual encoding theorem for finite-domain all-different systems. Positive synthetic residuals pin variables to finite allowed domains, while negative signed residuals reward pairwise inequality. For primes larger than the finite domain, the p-adic norm becomes an exact equality/inequality indicator on domain-respecting states, so the global minimisers of the constructed objective are exactly the minimum-conflict list-colourings, and in satisfiable cases the valid assignments.

Sudoku is included as a compact and familiar case study, not as a claim of a competitive Sudoku solver. The illustrative computations are intended only to show simple local search on the induced signed residual objective.

The manuscript builds on my earlier work on p-adic linear regression and NP-hardness of p-adic linear regression, but the central contribution here is different: a finite-domain all-different encoding theorem and its signed-residual interpretation.

Some of the background material and related signed p-adic constructions also appear in my doctoral thesis. The submitted article is the journal-form version of the finite-domain all-different/Sudoku construction, with the thesis material reduced and reframed around the paper's theorem.
